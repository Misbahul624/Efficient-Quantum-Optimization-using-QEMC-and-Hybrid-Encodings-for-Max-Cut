# Efficient-Quantum-Optimization-using-QEMC-and-Hybrid-Encodings-for-Max-Cut
Efficient Quantum Optimization using QEMC and Hybrid Encodings for Max-Cut
Combinatorial optimization problems such as the Max-Cut problem are well known for their computational complexity and wide applicability in areas including network design, machine learning, and operational research. Classical algorithms often struggle to efficiently solve these NP-hard problems, which has motivated the exploration of quantum computing approaches. Among these, the Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm (QAOA) has emerged as a promising framework; however, its practical implementation on Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (NISQ) devices is limited by high qubit requirements, circuit depth, and sensitivity to noise.

To address these challenges, this work proposes and investigates Quantum Encoding with Measurement Compression (QEMC), a novel approach designed to significantly reduce qubit usage while maintaining competitive solution quality. Unlike traditional methods that map each variable to a qubit, QEMC employs a compressed Hamiltonian representation using weighted Pauli operators, enabling logarithmic scaling in qubit requirements. In addition to QEMC, hybrid encoding strategies combining Jordan–Wigner and Bravyi–Kitaev transformations with measurement compression are explored to preserve encoding accuracy while managing computational resources.

A comprehensive experimental analysis is conducted using randomly generated graph instances ranging from small to large scales. The performance of QEMC is compared with QAOA and hybrid approaches based on metrics such as objective function value, computational runtime, qubit utilization, and stability. The results demonstrate that QEMC achieves superior scalability and efficiency, delivering high-quality solutions with significantly fewer qubits and faster convergence times. While hybrid methods provide accurate Hamiltonian representations, they incur additional computational overhead, and QAOA shows limitations in scalability and consistency for larger problem sizes.

Overall, the findings establish QEMC as a highly effective and scalable approach for solving large-scale combinatorial optimization problems in the NISQ era, offering an optimal balance between resource efficiency and solution performance. This work highlights the potential of compressed quantum representations as a key direction for advancing practical quantum optimization.
